# STS Research Question Pivot

## Why Pivot
The strongest original question is a crossover-boundary question (aero-dominated to delay-dominated). If no crossover appears in tested space, a stronger finalist path is to focus on regime-specific control limitations and model decision failure zones.

## Primary Question (Current Best Fit)
When do simplified thrust-vector-control models make incorrect stability decisions despite matched parameter tuning, and which control-limitation regimes (delay-limited, aero-limited, mixed-interaction, model-mismatch-limited) dominate across operating conditions?

## Secondary Question (Boundary Path)
At what operating conditions does prediction error transition from aerodynamic-dominated to delay-dominated, and how confident is that transition estimate?

## Novelty Angle For STS
- The contribution is not just a better simulation.
- The contribution is an experimentally testable control-limitation regime atlas.
- The atlas shows where model simplification fails in decision-relevant ways.

## What To Measure In Flight
- Stability mismatch: predicted stable/unstable versus observed stable/unstable.
- Delay-only and aero-only error contributions under matched perturbations.
- Regime labels by angular-rate bin and operating condition.

## Claim You Can Defend If Crossover Is Absent
Within tested operating conditions, delay-limited dynamics dominate overall, but distinct mixed-interaction and model-mismatch-limited zones reveal where simplified models fail to preserve correct stability decisions.

## Claim You Can Defend If Crossover Is Found
A measurable and confidence-bounded transition boundary separates aerodynamic-dominated and delay-dominated instability prediction regimes.
